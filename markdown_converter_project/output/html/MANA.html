Mandatory Accept

Last Modified: 2020-05-20 | Code: MANA

The Mandatory Accept function allows for a workflow to be established after the survey is exported to the client where designated client users must accept or decline a survey.

_

How to Set up a Survey Mandatory Accept

Use these steps:

  1. Go to Survey -> Active Survey Forms.
  2. Select the client and click on the Show Surveys button.
  3. Click the Edit Survey Properties button corresponding to the survey form you want to set Survey Acceptance for:

  1. Locate the Survey Acceptance Mode interface:

The acceptance from a client perspective has two options: accept or decline (object). If the Client objects to the results, the client needs to enter a valid reason for it. A dialogue with the MSP follows, where each party has a limited time window to answer and the client is limited to the number of objections:

NOTE:
In order to be able to make objections the client user must be a member of: Myst.Survey.RequestForAction_AcceptSurvey.Open.Allow
The members of Myst.Survey.RequestForAction_AcceptSurvey.Close.Allow have the ability to force accept at any time.

The Text Template

The default text template for the objection message prompt can be set differently for each survey and the default template is stored in the CMS system:

  1. Go to Administration -> Tools and Settings -> CMS menu option.
  2. In the Templates -> Templates for Notification Services Emails folder open the Data Entry Template for RFA – Mandatory Acceptdocument:

In the template you can define placeholders for some of the client data that are replaced by the system:

#$FIRSTNAME$#, #$LASTNAME$# - are replaced by the client's first and last name. #$EMAIL$# - email address.

Working Days Form

The function of this form is to set Default definition for Workdays / Holidays of the week and to set exception dates (days).

Under the Default Definition, check the specific days of the week that are to be considered as Workdays. Days that are not checked are considered holidays.

Exceptions to the Default Definition must be manually entered under the Exception Dates. To add an Exception Date select the Year, Month, and Day and whether it is being added as a Holiday or a Workday, then click the Add Exception button. All existing Exception Dates will be listed in the Date Exception table. Clicking on the  icon will remove an exception from the list.

Accept Procedure

A client user can view the survey's acceptance status from the report in Survey Explorer. Survey status is noted by icon:

  • Not answered

The survey is not reviewed. The client can either accept or decline the survey by posting objection.

  • Accepted

The survey is accepted without objection.

Objection

The survey is reviewed by the client and not accepted for some reason.

Objection Replied
 
The objection is reviewed by MSP and not accepted (or the problem is solved).

Auto Accepted

The survey is not accepted in the predefined time window and is automatically accepted by the system.

  • This icon in combination with  or  means that the survey is accepted after negotiation (with objection)
  • The survey is deactivated, can only be viewed from authorized staff for reference. Not included in the analysis or reports

Acceptance status can be changed by clicking on the Acceptance icon in the Survey Preview mode:

Here the client can choose to accept a survey by clicking the Accept button or decline the survey by clicking the Submit Objection button:

NOTE:
Being part of “Myst.Survey.RequestForAction_UnconditionallyAcceptSurvey.Close.Deny” security group regulates the visibility of the “Accept” button in the RFAs interfaces:

If the client chooses to decline a survey, they must state a reason for that and may propose a solution:

After submitting the objection, the client user is no longer allowed to change status or post comments until the MSP responds:

The survey must be accepted in the predefined time window or the status will be set to Auto Accepted.

The MSP can review and respond to the objection by clicking on the Acceptance icon or simply by clicking on the status icon in Survey Explorer.  

If the reason for the objection is valid, then the MSP may choose to disable the survey by clicking the Submit and deactivate survey button. Deactivated surveys are excluded from analysis and can no longer be viewed by the client.

The client may reply with a new objection until the maximum number of objections has been reached, after that the client may only accept it.

If the objection is not replied to in the predefined time window, then it is considered valid and the survey is deactivated.
